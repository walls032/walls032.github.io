About me:

Get to know my academic inspirations and aspirations

Hi there!

My name is Levi Walls (he/him), a first-generation student pursuing a PhD in Astronomy and Astrophysics at the University of Michigan advised by Prof. Ted Bergin at Michigan and Dr. Hui Li at Los Alamos National Laboratory. Having been fortunate enough to try many different research projects throughout my educational journey, which you can learn more about below, I have found my academic home in the protoplanetary disk and planet formation community. I am particularly inspired by the joint progress being made in the numerical techniques and theory of planet formation as well as the ever-growing number of protoplanetary disk observations with instruments like ALMA, NOEMA, and JWST. The detailed, global simulations of protostellar disks I run are aimed to build theoretical framework contextualizing the clues to the origins of planets that the disk observations are providing.

Research:

Tackling big questions in planet formation

These are some questions that have inspired and continue to inspire my research journey:

  • Can we trace back the chemical and dynamical history of water in protostellar disks?

  • How are organic molecules distributed throughout young disks?

  • What do the observational signatures of planetesimal nurseries tell us?

  • How does dust growth affect protoplanetary disks and vice versa?
